# Historical Notes

## Cook (1971)
Stephen Cook, "The Complexity of Theorem-Proving Procedures"
- Defined polynomial-time Turing reducibility
- Proved SAT is complete for NP
- Used tableau method (variables encode computation history)

## Levin (1973)  
Leonid Levin, "Universal Search Problems"
- Independently proved same result in Soviet Union
- Introduced "universal enumeration" technique
- Cook-Levin theorem jointly named

## Karp (1972)
Richard Karp, "Reducibility Among Combinatorial Problems"
- Refined to many-one reductions
- Catalogued 21 NP-complete problems
- Established the methodology of NP-completeness proofs

## Impact
- Cook won Turing Award (1982)
- Karp won Turing Award (1985)
- NP-completeness is now a standard tool in algorithm design
- P vs NP remains the central open problem in CS
